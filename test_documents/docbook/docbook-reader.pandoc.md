# Index terms {#indexterms}

In the simplest case, index terms[]{.indexterm primary="index term"}
consists of just a `<primary>` element, but []{.indexterm
primary="index term" secondary="multi-level"} they can also consist of a
`<primary>` and `<secondary>` element, and []{.indexterm
primary="index term" secondary="multi-level" tertiary="3-level"} can
even include a `<tertiary>` term.

Index terms can also refer to other index terms: []{.indexterm
primary="index cross referencing"}[]{.indexterm primary="index term"
secondary="cross references" see="index cross referencing"}exclusively,
using the `<see>` tag; or []{.indexterm
primary="index cross referencing" seealso="cross referencing"} as a
reference to related terms, using the `<seealso>` tag.

[]{.indexterm primary="food" secondary="big baguette supreme"}Nested
content in index term elements is flattened.
